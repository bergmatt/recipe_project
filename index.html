Custard Sauce

<p>I am a enthusiastic history buff and amateur cook. I will be listing some of my favorite recipes from the days of yore. To begin with, I'll start off with a simple custard sauce from eighteenth century England, although you will find that there is not much difference with modern cusatard sauces today. The addition of rose water, which adds a nice floral note tot he sauce, is the main difference you might notice with modern sauces of this kind.</p>

<h3>Ingredients</h3>
<ul>
    <li>4 egg yolks</li>
    <li>1/4 cup sugar</li>
    <li>pinch of salt</li>
    <li>1 3/4 cup milk</li>
    <li>1/4 cup heavy cream</li>
    <li>4 drops rose water</li>
    <li>1 1/2 tbsp brandy</li>
</ul>

<h3>Method</h3>
<ol>
    <li>In the top of a double boiler, beat the eggs yolks with the sugar and the salt.</li>
    <li>Add the milk and the cream and stir well.</li>
    <li>Set the pot over (not in) gently boiling water and cook, stirring constantly, until the custard is thick enough to coate the back of a spoon (about 10 minutes).</li>
    <li>Remove from heat and stir in the rose water and brandy</li>
</ol>

<p>Note: This sauce can be served hot or cold. It will thicken as it cools. </p>
